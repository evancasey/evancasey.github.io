Evan Casey

I am an aspiring artificial intelligence researcher.

I am broadly interested in the intersection between deep learning and reinforcement learning. My current research is focused on the problem of robotics in unconstrained environments. Specifically, I am interested in: (1) developing new techniques for improving sample efficiency and scalability (2) developing new methods for applying RL successfully to real world robotics tasks (eg. transfer learning, imitation, multi-task objectives). However, there are many interesting problems in machine learning and I hope to work on at least a few of them at some point.

Most recently, I spent 3 months at Recurse Center (formerly known as Hacker School) where I studied machine learning and neural networks. Before that, I was a software engineer at Tapad from June 2014 to February 2016 up through the acquisition by the European telecom giant Telenor for 360M. I worked on the petabyte-scale data infrastructure powering their real-time ad bidding systems and the Device Graph, a graph based representation of device ownership that uses a probabilistic model to infer the relationship between devices.

In my free time I like to skateboard, take photographs, and travel. You can find me elsewhere on the web on my blog, github, twitter, and linkedin.
